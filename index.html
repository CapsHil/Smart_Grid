About Data.

Considering an electric car with a 24 kWh battery that gets discharged by 6 kWh per day during the week (monday to friday) and given the fact the car has a 7 kW quick charge and has to be fully charged each morning before 6:00.

Calculate (based on the collective costs of energy for 2015):
- the total collective energy cost for the vehicle if the user recharges it between 7:00PM and 8:00PM
- the same cost if the battery gets charged according to the tariff division given in annex
- the total collective energy cost for the vehicle if charged during the best period each night between 7:00PM and 6:00AM

Conclusion.

Here are the final results of our work.

With those results we can see that the 7:00PM-8:00PM approach is the most expensive. This is due to the relatively high prices at this moment of the day. Charging the car when coming home may not the best idea.

When charging according to the division plan, there is a significant price drop. It's obviously a better option than charging during the 7:00PM-8:00PM interval. This is due to the cheaper prices during the night.

By charging the car during the best hour of the night we obviously get the best price we could possibly get. It illustrates the benefits of future Smart Home technologies, which will allow us to adapt and improve our energy consumption.
